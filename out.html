Verbs with Out

to finish, use, or sell all of something, so that there is none left
1. We've run out of milk.
2. They ran out of gas before they reached the gas station.
3. We're running out of stock.

to to discover, to learn, something
1. When did Melissa find out she was pregnant?
2. You will never find out the truth.
3. Her parents found out that she had a boyfriend.

used to warn someone to be vigilant
1. Watch out for cars when you're crossing the road.
2. We need to watch out for any problems that could arise.
3. Tourists must watch out for pickpockets in Barcelona.

to distribute/to give something to several people
1. McDonalds is giving out free hamburgers.
2. The teacher gave out the exam papers.
3. The company hasn't given out any information on the new product.

to resolve something (or) to excercise

1. We need to work out this problem quickly.
2. Paul and Sam have worked out their differences.
3. He works out at the gym every morning.

to faint

1. He passed out from the pain.
2. I'm going to pass out from the heat.
3. You could pass out if you don't eat.
