Phrasal Verb Master

Verbs with Into

to examine, to evaluate, to assess, to examine, to investigate
1. I'm looking into buying a new computer.
2. Tom will look into the problem.
3. We're looking into the cost of a new bathroom.

to become interested (or) involved in something
1. He is always getting into trouble.
2. I have gotten into yoga recently.
3. Let's not get into an argument with him.

to meet someone unexpectedly, crash into something
1. I ran into my ex-boyfriend at the party.
2. You never know who you will run into at the party.
3. He was driving too fast and ran into a wall.

to become, to transform
1. A caterpillar turns into a butterfly.
2. This sofa turns into a bed.
3. He has turned into a snob since he became rich.

to enter a place; (or) to discuss, describe, or explain something

1. He went into the building through the back door.
2. He will go into the details later.
3. We can't go into that issue now. Let's discuss it later.

to enter with force

1. The thief broke into the house.
2. They broke into my car and stole my wallet.
3. I had to break into my apartment because I lost my keys.
